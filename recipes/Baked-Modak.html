Baked Modak

Baking SIG hosted a Modak making station at Ganesh Chaturthi hosted by SLC. We experimented with the dish since it was the first time we were making modaks and ended up going with a baked version of it.

Author: Priyansha Ranjan

Ingredients

Dough

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup wheat flour
  • 1 tsp semolina (sooji)
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • Milk – as needed

Filling

  • 1 cup desiccated coconut
  • 1/2 cup jaggery
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • Dry fruits of your choice

Steps

1. Mix the dry ingredients and knead together with ghee and slightly warm milk to form a puri-like dough.

2. Heat ghee in a pan and lightly fry the desiccated coconut. Add jaggery on low heat and mix until the jaggery melts and the filling comes together. Cool the mixture, then add chopped dry fruits.

3. Use modak moulds to shape the dough and add the filling inside. You can also shape them by hand.

4. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Place the modaks on a baking tray and brush them with a milk and butter wash.

5. Bake for 30–40 minutes until the modaks are golden brown.
