Mutton Meatball Sub

While it was originally intended to be served to folks during a film screening of Welcome in the Amphitheatre, due to a last-minute rescheduling we had to give those away in my quad itself. It was the last mass cooking scene I did in my quad and it was quite a bittersweet affair. The sub was great though.

Author: Uday Khanna

Ingredients

  • Mutton/Chicken Meatballs (Pre-made or use this recipe)
  • French Baguette (Q-mart in Gachibowli has fresh ones — or bake your own!)

For the Marinara Sauce

  • San Marzano Canned Tomatoes
  • Crushed Garlic (there is never enough garlic)
  • Fresh Basil (1–2 bunches)
  • Baking Powder (a pinch)
  • Finely Chopped Onion (1:4 ratio to tomato)
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt, Oregano, Pepper, Chilli Flakes

Cheese

  • Parmesan
  • Smoked Cheddar
  • American Cheese Slices

Steps

Marinara Sauce

1. Cook crushed garlic and chilli flakes in olive oil until the garlic turns golden.

2. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.

3. Add crushed San Marzano tomatoes and salt. Let it cook until it thickens. Add water and slow cook the sauce.

4. Once bubbling, stir in a pinch of baking powder to cut the acidity.

5. Add oregano and adjust salt if needed.

6. Chiffonade the basil leaves and stir them into the sauce.

Assembling the Meatball Sub

1. Shallow fry the meatballs in olive oil until cooked and golden.

2. Slit a baguette in half (keeping one end intact) and toast the inside with butter until golden.

3. Layer smoked cheddar on the toasted bread, then add American cheese slices and place the meatballs over them.

4. Pour hot marinara sauce over the meatballs.

5. Grate Parmesan cheese on top and garnish with more chiffonade basil.

6. Close that baby up and enjoy the comforting, cheesy, meaty goodness!
