Misal

Misal is a very favourite snack where I come from (Pune) and reminiscent of home. This was the first Maharashtrian dish that I prepared in ISB and also the first time that I cooked for and hosted 12-15 people in my quad! This dish is special to me because I could give my close friends a taste of the flavours I had grown up with and they could enjoy it along with me.

Author: Madhura Potnis

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup grated, dry coconut
  • Oil
  • 2 big slices Onion, 1 big onion (finely chopped for the potato sabzi)
  • 4–5 Garlic cloves
  • 1” finely chopped Ginger
  • 1 tbsp Coriander powder
  • 1 tbsp Goda masala
  • 1 tbsp Red chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp Kanda Lasun masala (Maharashtra special masala)
  • 250 gms Mataki (moth beans)
  • 2–3 big potatoes
  • Curry leaves
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 4 glasses Water
  • Salt to taste
  • A little Jaggery
  • Farsan
  • Finely chopped onion (for garnishing)
  • Finely chopped coriander leaves (for garnishing)
  • Fine Shev

Steps

1. Heat up a pan on medium heat. Add coconut and roast until it gets a reddish color.

2. When done, take it out into a blender pot.

3. In the same pan add 1 tsp oil. Add sliced onion, garlic, and ginger.

4. Fry on medium heat until onion gets reddish color for about 5-6 minutes.

5. Turn off the gas and when the mixture cools down, transfer it into the blender jar with the roasted coconut.

6. Blend the mixture really well.

7. Heat up 1 tbsp oil in a pan. Add blended masala and fry on medium heat for about 3-4 minutes.

8. Add coriander powder, goda masala, kanda lasun masala and red chili powder.

9. If you don’t have goda masala, you can add kitchen king masala or any other garam masala.

10. Mix well and fry until the masala releases oil.

11. Take mataki, add water, turmeric powder, and salt.

12. Cook on medium heat until 1 whistle. Do not overcook the mataki.

13. Add mataki along with water into the masala. Mix well. Add more water if needed.

14. You can also cook mataki in the kat itself.

15. Add salt and jaggery to taste. Boil misal on medium for 7-8 minutes.

16. Turn off the gas. Kat for misal is ready.

17. Make potato sabzi – boil potatoes, then peel and chop. In a pan, make tadka of mustard seeds, jeera, hing, turmeric, curry leaves. Add garlic, ginger, and green chilli paste. Add chopped onion. Once onion is soft, add potato pieces and sauté for 4-5 minutes. Add salt to taste.

18. While serving, in a bowl add one layer of mataki at the base. Put a layer of potato sabzi on it.

19. Add lots of farsan and kat of misal.

20. Add chopped onion and coriander leaves.

21. Garnish with fine shev. Serve with pav, onion, coriander, and a slice of lemon.
