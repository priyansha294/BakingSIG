Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

This was my recipe for the Bandhan Bake Sale where we fed an insane amount of the campus and raised 1 lakh in 2 days.

Author: Uday Khanna

Ingredients

  • 200g Brown Sugar
  • 100g White Sugar
  • 340g Butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 ½ tsp Vanilla Paste or 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 11.5 oz All-Purpose Flour
  • ½ tsp Baking Soda
  • ½ tsp Baking Powder
  • 1 Tbsp Kosher Salt
  • 1 Tbsp Coffee Powder
  • 340g Chocolate Chips (can sub in gems, nuts, etc. within this weight)

Steps

1. Prepare the Butter: Use room temperature butter or brown the butter for extra flavor. If browning, increase to 2 ½ sticks to compensate for moisture loss. Brown on medium heat for 4–6 minutes, stirring constantly. Let cool and return to room temp.

2. Cream Butter & Sugar: Beat brown and white sugar with butter on medium to medium-high speed for 1–3 minutes until fluffy. Scrape down the sides between steps.

3. Mix in Eggs & Vanilla: Add eggs and vanilla, beat 30 seconds to 1 minute. If using hazelnut coffee powder, add now.

4. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, and coffee powder.

5. Combine Dry & Wet: Mix the dry ingredients into the wet until just combined — don’t overmix.

6. Scrape & Fold: Make sure no flour or butter pockets are left. Fold by hand if needed.

7. Add “Stuff”: Fold in chocolate chips or other add-ins of choice.

8. Portion & Chill: Scoop dough (#18 or #20 scoop size), place on tray, cover with wrap, and chill for at least 2 hours. Overnight or longer is better!

9. Bake: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake straight from fridge on cold tray. 12–13 minutes worked best. Slightly press the dough balls down before baking for even cooking.
