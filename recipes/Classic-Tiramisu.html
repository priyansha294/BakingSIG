Classic Tiramisu

This was the Baking SIG's first proper cooking workshop and we started with a classic tiramisu, conducting the workshop across two days in 4 batches. We had from absolute novices to pro home cooks participating and each taking away if not a new skill then at least a tasty dessert.

Author: Uday Khanna

Ingredients

  • 3 medium eggs
  • 2 tbsp castor sugar
  • 250g mascarpone cheese, softened
  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee, cooled to room temperature
  • 3 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 16-24 ladyfinger cookies (savoiardi)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
  • Dark chocolate shavings or cocoa nibs (optional)

Steps

1. Brew coffee and cool it in a large bowl, once cooled add liqueur, this is optional.

2. Separate 3 egg whites in a clean bowl and use a hand mixer to bring them to stiff peaks.

3. Add a tablespoon of sugar slowly as you whisk. (You can add some lemon before whisking to help stabilize them.)

4. In a separate bowl, combine 3 egg yolks and 1 tablespoon of sugar till the ribbon stage.

5. Combine the mascarpone with the yolk mixture until fully mixed, taking care not to overmix.

6. Sacrifice 1/3 of the whipped egg whites into the yolk mixture in a large bowl, then add the remaining 2/3 taking care to just fold it in.

7. Assemble your tiramisu alternating with a layer of coffee-dipped ladyfingers and cream mixture. Then after the last layer of cream, coat it with a layer of cocoa.

8. Add chocolate shavings if interested and let it cool for at least 4 hrs or ideally overnight.
